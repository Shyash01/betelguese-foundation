The Principles Behind Our Work

Every visit, every distribution, every conversation is guided by these three commitments — not as policy, but as practice.

Compassion in Practice

We meet people where they are — with patience and warmth, so support feels like care, not a checklist.

Dignity by Design

Whether sharing a meal or sharing time, we protect agency, privacy, and respect in how help is offered and received.

Collective Action

Lasting change grows from neighbours, volunteers, and partners moving together — not from a single moment on camera.

Elderly care & companionship

Dua Care Centre Initiative

We organized a dedicated visit to the Dua Care Centre old age home to engage with and support the elderly. This social drive focused on fostering compassion, meaningful interaction, and emotional well-being.

Our team spent time connecting with residents through conversations and activities, bringing comfort and companionship while reinforcing the importance of empathy within the community.

Emotional Well-being Community Connection Elderly Support
Food security & relief

Noida Food Donation Drive

Aimed at supporting underprivileged and vulnerable communities across Noida, our team distributed freshly prepared meals and essential food supplies to individuals in need, ensuring access to basic nutrition.

Carried out with a strict focus on dignity, compassion, and efficient distribution, this drive reached multiple areas — reflecting our commitment to reducing hunger and supporting grassroots community well-being.

Environmental sustainability

Community Plantation Drive

As part of our commitment to ecological balance, we conducted a neighbourhood plantation drive — planting saplings in accessible public spaces and encouraging local residents to actively participate.

Alongside the physical planting, we promoted awareness regarding tree conservation and individual environmental responsibility, connecting community members to the larger climate conversation.

Ecological Balance Community Participation Environmental Awareness
Civic technology & safety

Cyber First Responder Programme

In collaboration with CyberPeace Foundation and supported by Google.org, we trained 250+ students in cybersecurity awareness — equipping them to recognize digital threats, protect personal data, and support their communities online.

This programme represents our belief that digital safety is a civic right — and that young people, when properly equipped, become the first line of defence for their families and institutions.

The Betelgeuse Grassroots Fellowship

We offer meaningful, field-driven internship opportunities for students and freshers in the Delhi NCR region. Step out of the classroom and into the community.

Hands-On Fieldwork

You are in the community distributing resources, coordinating logistics, and planning outreach — not at a desk.

Meaningful Mentorship

Work directly with experienced social workers and operations leads who understand the non-profit sector.

Flexible Engagement

Short-term, long-term, online, and offline participation models to accommodate student schedules.

Career-Ready Outcomes

Earn a verified certificate of internship, a performance-based LOR, and practical leadership experience.

Application Protocol

We welcome college students, freshers, and passionate citizens. Here is how to begin:

1
Prepare

Prepare your updated CV or Resume and a brief personal statement.

2
Write

Draft a short cover email stating your background, why you want to join, and your preferred dates and format (online or offline).

3
Apply

Email your application to internships@betelgeusefoundation.org with the subject line: Internship Application — [Your Name].

Our team reviews applications on a rolling basis. Shortlisted candidates are contacted within 3–5 working days for an orientation call.
